Dear Headway hiring team,

I'm applying for the Principal PM role because Headway's mission — making mental healthcare accessible and affordable for everyone — is the kind of problem I want to spend the next chapter of my career on. One in four Americans has a treatable condition; most can't afford care. You've already solved the hardest infrastructure problem. What draws me now is the product layer on top: helping a million patients a month actually get into care, stay in care, and feel better. That's individual impact at a scale few companies can offer, and it's exactly where I do my best work.

My career has been defined by that tension — deeply personal user moments, delivered through platforms that reach millions. At Coursera, I launched the Gen AI Coach from zero to 2M+ learners and 40M+ interactions, with disproportionate adoption by underserved groups: 11% more women, 40% more career starters, 10% more non-degreed learners. I didn't optimize for aggregate engagement; I designed for the learner sitting alone at 11pm, stuck on a concept, needing someone who gets it. The platform scaled because the individual experience was right. Before that, I built AI translation infrastructure that made 4,600+ courses available in 21 languages — 1.5M enrollments from learners who previously had no path in. At WEX, I lead enterprise innovation with Board-level visibility, founding an AI-native incubator and coaching six pilot teams on a new way of building. Across every role, the pattern is the same: find the human job to be done, build something that feels personal, then platform it so the impact compounds.

That's why I didn't wait for an interview to start thinking about Headway's patient experience. I walked the current onboarding journey end to end, documented every step, and mapped the emotional arc from landing page to first free consultation booking. I wanted to understand not just where users drop off, but why — the eligibility ambiguity, the identity fatigue, the choice anxiety when faced with dozens of therapist profiles. Then I went further: I sketched a product vision for where the patient journey could go, grounded in a simple north star — therapy is about positive state change, moving patients from emotionally closed to emotionally open.
